Issues¶

Data Quality¶

  • Missing entries in reactions(User ID, Type) table
  • Wrong datatype for datetime in reactions table
  • Multiple representations of category data entry in content table - sometimes quotes, sometimes no quotes, sometimes lowercase, sometimes uppercase
  • Use of non-unique column names with varied values in content table(Type) and reactions/reactiontypes(Type) table
  • Non-descriptive column headers(Type in Content table and Type in Reactions table
  • Inconsistent column headers naming format- sometimes lower case, sometimes upper case(URL), whitespace in column headers (User ID)

Tidiness¶

  • reactions, reactiontypes and location tables should be part of content table
    • Extraneous columns

Insights¶

By performing an analysis of your data we saw that you had a total of 16 unique categories of posts across your sample dataset. This includes themes such as Animals, Science and food. We also found out that there were 1897 reactions from just the animal category. People clearly love to post about animals! It makes sense that Users engaged more with the animals post using a heart

And also the most posts happened in January; 17th January precisely.This aligns with seasonal trends of social media users feeling the need to reconnect with people more after calendar events like Christmas

Back to the main question... what were the top 5 most popular categories of posts?

From our analysis, it can be seen from the bar chart that the top 5 most popular categories of posts were animals, science, healthy eating, technology and food in descending order. Animals had an aggregate popularity score of around 74,965. It is very interesting to see both food and healthy eating within the top 5, it really shows that food is a highly engaging content category. Healthy eating ranks slightly higher than food, so perhaps your user base may be skewed towards healthy eaters and health-conscious people. Finally, its also interesting to see science and technology too. This may suggest that people enjoy consuming factual content and snippets of content that they can learn something from

In business terms, this could suggest that the most popular category, animals, is tailing away from the rest of the categories and may continue to get more and more popular. To avoid an issue where one content category consumes the entire platform, it will be important for you to ensure that any algorithms used to govern the content on the platform gives a fair balance to the content categories.
